      <div class="card article">
        <h3>What is Hydrocephalus?</h3>
        <p>Hydrocephalus is a condition in which cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) builds up in the brain’s ventricles, leading to increased pressure.</p>

        <h3>Common Symptoms</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Increase in head size (infants)</li>
          <li>Bulging fontanelle / scalp vein prominence</li>
          <li>Vomiting, irritability, excessive sleepiness</li>
          <li>Headache / blurred vision (older children)</li>
          <li>Developmental delay / feeding issues</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>How It’s Evaluated</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Clinical exam + head circumference tracking</li>
          <li>Ultrasound (infants), CT/MRI when needed</li>
          <li>Assess possible causes (congenital, infection, hemorrhage, tumor, etc.)</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>Common Treatment Options</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt</li>
          <li>Endoscopic Third Ventriculostomy (ETV) (selected cases)</li>
        </ul>
